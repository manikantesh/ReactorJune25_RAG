CASE SUMMARY

Case Name: John Doe v. State of California
Court: Supreme Court of California
Date: January 15, 2020
Citation: S123456

Summary:
John Doe sued the State of California for breach of contract after the State failed to pay for services rendered under a written agreement. The trial court found in favor of Doe, awarding $100,000 in damages. On appeal, the Supreme Court of California affirmed the judgment, holding that the State's failure to pay was a material breach and that sovereign immunity did not bar recovery. The damages were found to be appropriate and supported by the evidence.

Key Issues:
- Material breach of contract
- Sovereign immunity
- Damages assessment

Holding:
Judgment for the plaintiff affirmed.
